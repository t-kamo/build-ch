# Episode001 Video Prototype Storyboard

Purpose:

Define the first short-form video prototype workflow for Episode001 Hidden Waterfall House.

Target platforms:

- TikTok
- YouTube Shorts
- Instagram Reels

Target length:

15-25 seconds

Core transformation:

Natural Waterfall -> Hidden Anomaly -> Early Excavation -> Expanded Excavation -> Workspace -> Hidden Dwelling

---

# Opening Hook

Show the untouched waterfall first, then quickly reveal that something unusual exists behind it.

Viewer question:

"Is there something hidden behind that waterfall?"

Hook target:

0-3 seconds

---

# Recommended Pacing

Use fast visual progression with no dead time.

Suggested rhythm:

- Stage A: 2 seconds
- Stage B: 3 seconds
- Stage C: 3 seconds
- Stage D: 4 seconds
- Stage E: 4 seconds
- Stage F: 5 seconds

Total:

21 seconds

Each stage should feel like a clear visual upgrade from the previous stage.

---

# Recommended Music Style

Primary:

- Low atmospheric cinematic pulse
- Natural waterfall ambience
- Subtle construction ASMR layers

Avoid:

- Loud music that hides work sounds
- Overly dramatic trailer music
- Comedy or meme-style audio

Audio direction:

Let the waterfall ambience continue through the full video, then add subtle tool sounds during excavation stages and warmer ambience during the final reveal.

---

# Scene 01 — Natural Waterfall

## Input Image

Stage A

## Duration

2 seconds

## Visual Purpose

Establish a beautiful untouched natural waterfall in a remote forest.

No hidden structure should be obvious yet.

## Viewer Emotion

Curiosity and visual attraction.

## Transition Logic

Start with a calm static shot. Hold long enough for the viewer to understand the location, then transition into the subtle anomaly.

## Kling Image-to-Video Prompt

Cinematic static shot of a massive natural waterfall in a remote forest, dark volcanic cliff, dense moss and vegetation, soft mist near the pool, photorealistic style.

The camera remains locked in the same elevated front-facing position.

Water flows naturally with heavy realistic motion. Mist drifts subtly near the pool. Leaves move gently in the air.

No people. No construction. No artificial objects. No entrance should be emphasized.

Natural waterfall ambience and soft forest atmosphere.

No cuts. No zoom. No camera movement.

---

# Scene 02 — Hidden Anomaly

## Input Image

Stage B

## Duration

3 seconds

## Visual Purpose

Reveal a subtle dark geological recess behind or near the waterfall without making it too obvious.

## Viewer Emotion

Suspicion and curiosity.

## Transition Logic

Continue from the same waterfall composition. The viewer should notice a small abnormal shadow only after a moment.

## Kling Image-to-Video Prompt

Cinematic static shot of the same massive natural waterfall and volcanic cliff.

The waterfall remains the main visual focus. A subtle dark geological recess is partially hidden behind the moving water and surrounding rock.

Animate only natural environmental motion: water flow, mist, tiny leaf movement, and slight shifting shadows.

The hidden recess should not become the focal point. It should feel like a natural anomaly that most viewers notice only after looking closely.

No people. No tools. No construction markings. No artificial structures.

Natural waterfall ambience with quiet tension.

No cuts. No zoom. No camera movement.

---

# Scene 03 — Early Excavation

## Input Image

Stage C

## Duration

3 seconds

## Visual Purpose

Show that someone has just started digging behind the waterfall.

The opening should remain small, primitive, and believable.

## Viewer Emotion

Discovery and anticipation.

## Transition Logic

Move from hidden anomaly to first clear evidence of human effort. This is the first construction beat.

## Kling Image-to-Video Prompt

Cinematic static shot of the same waterfall and cliff environment.

A single worker wearing a grey hoodie, black baseball cap, and black work pants performs early hand excavation at the small recess behind the waterfall.

The scene plays as a fast realistic timelapse. The worker removes small loose stones and scrapes rough volcanic rock by hand tools, creating only a shallow early excavation.

The opening remains small, primitive, and unfinished. It should look like the first digging progress by one person over time.

Strong ASMR emphasis on rock scraping, shovel dragging, loose stones falling, footsteps on wet ground, and waterfall ambience.

No sudden large tunnel. No chamber. No interior room. No artificial structures. No camera movement. No cuts.

---

# Scene 04 — Expanded Excavation

## Input Image

Stage D

## Duration

4 seconds

## Visual Purpose

Show meaningful progress: the excavation has expanded into a usable work area.

## Viewer Emotion

Commitment and momentum.

## Transition Logic

Escalate from small early digging to a larger but still rough excavation. The build should feel like time has passed.

## Kling Image-to-Video Prompt

Cinematic static shot of the same waterfall, cliff, and forest environment.

A single worker wearing a grey hoodie, black baseball cap, and black work pants continues the excavation as a fast realistic timelapse.

The worker breaks small sections of rock, clears rubble, carries stones away, and gradually expands the rough opening into a usable work area.

The excavation remains physically believable for one person over time. The space is larger than before but still rough, unfinished, and carved into natural volcanic rock.

Strong ASMR emphasis on pickaxe hits, shovel scraping, falling gravel, stone movement, wet footsteps, and constant waterfall ambience.

No instant cave system. No luxury interior. No furniture. No lighting fixtures. No support beams unless already visible in the input image.

No cuts. No transitions. No zoom. No camera movement.

---

# Scene 05 — Habitable Unfinished Workspace

## Input Image

Stage E

## Duration

4 seconds

## Visual Purpose

Show that the hidden space is now usable, but still hand-built and unfinished.

## Viewer Emotion

Progress satisfaction and "this is becoming real."

## Transition Logic

Shift from raw excavation to practical living preparation. This is the bridge before the final reveal.

## Kling Image-to-Video Prompt

Cinematic static shot of the same hidden waterfall excavation area.

A single worker wearing a grey hoodie, black baseball cap, and black work pants works inside the rough hidden space as a fast realistic timelapse.

The worker organizes the floor, places simple hand-built wooden platforms, moves practical supplies, and improves the space into a habitable but unfinished workspace.

The natural volcanic rock remains dominant. The environment should feel self-built, practical, and in progress.

Strong ASMR emphasis on wood placement, tool impacts, dragging materials, footsteps, small stones shifting, and waterfall ambience outside.

No luxury furniture. No polished interior design. No sudden completed residence. No modern architecture. No large scale jump.

No cuts. No transitions. No zoom. Keep camera stable.

---

# Scene 06 — Final Reveal

## Input Image

Stage F v1

## Duration

5 seconds

## Visual Purpose

Deliver the emotional payoff: a completed hidden dwelling integrated into the waterfall environment.

## Viewer Emotion

Satisfaction, surprise, and fantasy living.

## Transition Logic

Transition from unfinished workspace to completed hidden dwelling. Hold the reveal long enough for viewers to absorb the final result.

## Kling Image-to-Video Prompt

Cinematic reveal shot of the completed hidden waterfall dwelling integrated into the same natural waterfall and volcanic cliff environment.

The waterfall continues flowing in front of the hidden home. Warm interior light glows softly inside the hand-built dwelling. Natural stone, wood, and practical living details feel integrated with the cliff.

Use subtle environmental motion: flowing water, drifting mist, flickering warm light, and slight leaf movement.

The final space should feel desirable, believable, and hidden, as if one person gradually built it over time.

Audio should combine waterfall ambience, soft interior warmth, and subtle satisfying reveal music.

No excessive camera movement. No fast zoom. No flashy transition. Preserve the same environment and scale.

---

# Final Reveal

The final reveal should communicate:

- This was hidden behind the waterfall
- The build is now complete
- The dwelling feels warm, usable, and desirable
- The natural environment remains part of the home

The viewer should think:

"I wish this place existed."

---

# Validation Checklist

Before accepting the prototype:

- Runtime is between 15 and 25 seconds
- Stage A reads as untouched nature
- Stage B anomaly is subtle but visible
- Stage C feels like early excavation, not a pre-existing cave
- Stage D shows expanded excavation without unrealistic scale jump
- Stage E is habitable but unfinished
- Stage F delivers a satisfying hidden dwelling reveal
- Same waterfall is recognizable throughout
- Same cliff and vegetation are recognizable throughout
- No sudden luxury jump before Stage F
- No random camera shifts
- No confusing construction logic
- Audio supports waterfall ambience and construction ASMR
- Final reveal is held long enough to feel satisfying

---

# Notes

This first prototype is designed to test whether the 6-stage transformation structure creates a clear and satisfying short-form video.

Do not optimize for perfection yet.

Optimize for:

Curiosity -> Progress -> Transformation -> Reveal
