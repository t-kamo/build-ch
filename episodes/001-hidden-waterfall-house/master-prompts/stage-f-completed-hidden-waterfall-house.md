# Stage F — Completed Hidden Waterfall House Master Prompt

## Purpose

Transform the approved Stage E image into Stage F.

Stage F is the final reveal.

The hidden dwelling should now feel complete, desirable, and naturally integrated into the waterfall environment.

## Stage F Goal

Completed hidden dwelling integrated into the waterfall environment.

The viewer should feel:

"I wish this place existed."

## Optimization Target

Video storytelling progression.

The final reveal should feel earned after Stage A through Stage E.

---

## 1. Nano Banana Prompt

Use the approved Stage E image as the base image.

Preserve the same waterfall identity.

Preserve the same dark volcanic rock identity.

Preserve the same natural stone context.

Preserve the same spatial relationship to the entrance.

Preserve the same camera logic and believable scale.

Keep the completed dwelling visually connected to the waterfall through the entrance, mist, reflected daylight, water movement, visible falling water, or natural rock openings.

Transform the habitable unfinished space into a completed luxury hidden waterfall residence. Add warm layered interior lighting, premium but grounded furniture, natural stone integration, refined wood flooring, subtle glass accents, soft textiles, comfortable seating, built-in storage, and a calm premium atmosphere. Preserve visible volcanic rock walls and natural stone features as part of the design.

The space should feel built into the waterfall cliff, not placed inside a generic apartment.

The luxury should feel realistic, grounded, warm, and desirable.

The completed dwelling should still show its origin as a hidden space carved gradually into dark volcanic rock.

Do not make it futuristic.

Do not make it a sci-fi bunker.

Do not make it a fantasy palace.

Do not make it look like a generic hotel room or apartment.

Do not remove all natural rock.

Do not make the interior impossibly huge.

Do not lose the waterfall connection.

The final image should show a completed hidden waterfall house that feels real, premium, naturally integrated, and emotionally satisfying.

---

## 2. Validation Checklist

- Stage E waterfall identity remains connected
- Natural volcanic rock remains visible
- Space feels complete and desirable
- Warm layered lighting is present
- Furniture feels premium but grounded
- Wood, stone, glass, and textiles feel realistic
- Scale remains believable
- Waterfall context remains visible or strongly implied
- Final reveal feels earned from previous construction stages
- No sci-fi bunker look
- No fantasy palace look
- No generic apartment look
- Natural rock identity is not removed
- Interior is not impossibly huge
- Viewer wants to be there

---

## 3. Failure Modes

### Generic Luxury Room

The result looks like a normal apartment, hotel room, or generic luxury interior without waterfall identity.

### Fantasy Overdesign

The model creates an impossible palace, glowing fantasy cave, sci-fi bunker, or unrealistic mega-room.

### Natural Context Loss

The waterfall, volcanic rock, mist, water reflections, or natural entrance relationship disappears.

### Scale Failure

The interior becomes too huge, too tall, too wide, or inconsistent with the prior excavation.

### Material Failure

Materials look plastic, fake, over-polished, or inconsistent with a real hidden waterfall residence.

### Unearned Reveal

The final image does not feel connected to Stage E and appears as a sudden unrelated interior.

---

## 4. Retry Prompt

Use the previous image as the base image.

Keep the waterfall identity, volcanic rock, entrance relationship, natural stone context, camera logic, and scale consistent.

Reduce fantasy, sci-fi, and generic apartment elements.

Preserve visible natural volcanic rock.

Preserve the feeling that this dwelling was built gradually inside the waterfall cliff.

Add grounded luxury only: warm layered lighting, realistic wood flooring, comfortable furniture, subtle glass accents, stone integration, soft textiles, and refined storage.

Keep the waterfall visually connected through mist, reflected daylight, visible falling water, natural rock openings, or water reflections.

The final image should feel like a real completed hidden waterfall house, not a fantasy palace, sci-fi bunker, or generic luxury room.
