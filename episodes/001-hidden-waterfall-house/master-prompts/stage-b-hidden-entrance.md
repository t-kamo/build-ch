# Stage B — Hidden Entrance Master Prompt

## Purpose

Transform the approved Stage A master image into Stage B.

Stage B should show a hidden entrance behind the waterfall, but most viewers should not notice it immediately.

## Stage B Goal

A very subtle natural tunnel opening exists behind the waterfall.

The entrance should look like a naturally enlarged crack in dark volcanic rock.

The opening must be:

- Small
- Believable
- Primitive
- Unfinished
- Partially hidden by falling water and mist

## Optimization Target

Most viewers do not notice the entrance on first glance.

---

## 1. Nano Banana Prompt

Use the approved Stage A master image as the base image.

Preserve the exact waterfall.

Preserve the exact dark volcanic cliff.

Preserve the exact vegetation.

Preserve the exact mist.

Preserve the exact terrain.

Preserve the exact lighting direction.

Preserve the exact camera position.

Preserve the exact scale.

Keep everything else exactly the same.

Change only a tiny section of dark volcanic rock partially hidden behind the falling waterfall.

Add a very subtle small natural tunnel opening near the far edge of the waterfall, not in the center of the composition. Place it where the falling water, mist, shadow, and a protruding rock partially obscure it. The opening should look like a naturally enlarged vertical crack in the volcanic rock, not a constructed entrance. It should be dark, rough, primitive, unfinished, and only barely large enough for one adult person to crouch through.

The opening should not be obvious and should not become the focal point. It should blend into the shadows, wet rock texture, falling water, and waterfall mist. Most viewers should notice only the beautiful natural waterfall at first glance.

Do not create a large cave.

Do not create a visible room.

Do not add lighting.

Do not add furniture.

Do not add support beams.

Do not add doors.

Do not add construction markings.

Do not add paint, chalk, spray lines, flags, stakes, or measurement marks.

Do not add tools.

Do not add workers.

Do not add artificial structures.

Do not change the waterfall, cliff, vegetation, terrain, mist, lighting, camera position, or scale.

Final image should look almost identical to Stage A, except for a barely noticeable natural dark crack-like tunnel opening hidden behind the waterfall.

---

## 2. Validation Checklist

- Same waterfall shape as Stage A
- Same cliff geometry as Stage A except for the tiny hidden opening
- Same vegetation and moss placement
- Same terrain and pool area
- Same mist density and water spray
- Same soft daylight direction
- Same static elevated front-facing camera
- Same scale
- Opening is small and subtle
- Opening is positioned near the edge of the waterfall, not centered
- Opening is partially hidden behind falling water or mist
- Opening is partially obscured by rock, shadow, water, or mist
- Waterfall remains the dominant subject
- Opening looks like a natural volcanic rock crack
- Opening is rough, primitive, and unfinished
- No visible interior room
- No lighting
- No furniture
- No support beams
- No doors
- No construction markings
- No artificial structures
- Most viewers would not notice the opening immediately

---

## 3. Failure Modes

### Entrance Too Obvious

The opening becomes the main subject, too bright, too centered, too clean, or too clearly shaped.

### Entrance Centered

The opening appears in the middle of the waterfall or composition and becomes the focal point.

### Entrance Too Large

The opening becomes a large cave mouth, archway, room opening, or full walk-in tunnel.

### Artificial Entrance

The opening looks carved, framed, rectangular, engineered, or architectural.

### Interior Reveal

The model reveals a room, hallway, lit cave, living space, furniture, or finished interior.

### Construction Marking Regression

The model adds paint, chalk, spray lines, guide marks, flags, stakes, tools, or workers.

### Environment Drift

The waterfall, cliff shape, vegetation, mist, lighting, camera position, terrain, or scale changes from Stage A.

---

## 4. Retry Prompt if the Entrance Becomes Too Large

Use the previous image as the base image.

Keep the waterfall, cliff, vegetation, mist, terrain, lighting, camera position, and scale exactly the same.

Reduce the tunnel opening significantly.

Move visual emphasis away from the opening. The waterfall must remain the dominant subject.

Make the opening look like a narrow natural dark crack near the edge of the waterfall, mostly hidden behind falling water, protruding rock, shadow, and mist.

The opening should be barely noticeable, primitive, rough, and unfinished.

It should not look like a cave entrance, doorway, arch, room opening, or constructed passage.

Do not show any interior.

Do not add lights, beams, doors, furniture, markings, tools, workers, or artificial structures.

The final image should look almost identical to the original natural waterfall image, with only a subtle shadow-like crack behind the water.
