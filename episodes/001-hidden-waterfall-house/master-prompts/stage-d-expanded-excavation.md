# Stage D — Expanded Excavation Master Prompt

## Purpose

Transform the approved Stage C image into Stage D.

Stage D should show that the hidden dwelling is being actively created over time.

The space should become a usable work area, but it must remain raw, unfinished, and physically believable for a single person.

## Stage D Goal

Expanded excavation and usable work area.

The viewer should feel:

"This hidden place is being dug out slowly."

## Optimization Target

Video storytelling progression.

Stage D should clearly progress beyond shallow digging without jumping to a finished chamber or luxury interior.

---

## 1. Nano Banana Prompt

Use the approved Stage C image as the base image.

Preserve the same waterfall identity.

Preserve the same dark volcanic cliff.

Preserve the same vegetation.

Preserve the same mist and water spray.

Preserve the same lighting direction.

Preserve the same camera logic and scale.

Keep the build visually connected to the waterfall environment.

Change only the existing shallow excavation behind the waterfall.

Expand the shallow hand-dug recess into a deeper rough work cavity. The opening should remain primitive and unfinished, but the excavation should now be large enough for one person to crouch inside and work. Show a short, rough, human-scale cavity carved into dark volcanic rock. Add more fresh broken rock edges, small rubble piles, damp debris, scraped uneven stone floor, and rough tool-like shaping marks.

The space should feel like slow manual excavation progress over time, not an instant cave discovery.

The excavation volume must remain believable for one person working gradually.

Do not create a large chamber.

Do not create a finished room.

Do not create a luxury interior.

Do not add furniture.

Do not add beds.

Do not add sofas.

Do not add a kitchen.

Do not add glass.

Do not add decorative lighting.

Do not add polished floors.

Do not add finished wall panels.

Do not add modern architecture.

Do not add support beams unless they are extremely minimal, temporary, and rough.

Do not add doors.

Do not add artificial structures.

Do not change the waterfall, cliff, vegetation, mist, lighting, camera logic, or scale.

The final image should show a raw expanded excavation behind the waterfall that feels achievable by one person over time, with no sudden luxury jump.

---

## 2. Validation Checklist

- Stage C waterfall identity is preserved

- No large chamber

- No glass
- No decorative lighting
- No polished surfaces
- No modern architecture
- Progress feels gradual, not instant

---

## 3. Failure Modes

### Over-Excavation

The model creates a huge cave, full chamber, multi-room interior, or impossible excavation volume.

### Luxury Jump

The model adds furniture, warm final lighting, glass, polished floors, decor, or finished interior design.

### Pre-existing Cave Narrative

The image feels like someone discovered a natural cave instead of slowly creating a hidden dwelling.

### Artificial Overbuilding

The model adds walls, doors, beams, stairs, concrete, windows, lights, or architectural structures too early.

### Environment Disconnect

The waterfall, mist, cliff, vegetation, lighting, camera, or scale no longer matches Stage C.

### No Progress

The excavation remains too similar to Stage C and does not show expanded work area.

---

## 4. Retry Prompt

Use the previous image as the base image.

Keep the waterfall, cliff, vegetation, mist, water spray, lighting direction, camera logic, and scale exactly the same.

Remove any luxury, furniture, glass, polished floor, decorative lighting, finished walls, doors, stairs, or modern architecture.

Reduce any oversized cave or large chamber.

Show only a deeper rough human-scale excavation behind the waterfall, as if one person has slowly dug further into the volcanic rock.

The space should be a raw work cavity, not a finished room.

Add fresh broken rock texture, damp debris, small rubble piles, and a rough uneven floor.

The final image should feel like believable expanded excavation progress, not a completed interior.
