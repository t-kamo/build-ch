# Stage E — Habitable Unfinished Space Master Prompt

## Purpose

Transform the approved Stage D image into Stage E.

Stage E should show that the excavation is becoming a future dwelling.

The space should be clearly habitable but unfinished.

## Stage E Goal

Clearly habitable but unfinished living space.

The viewer should feel:

"This rough hidden excavation is starting to become a place someone could live."

## Optimization Target

Video storytelling progression.

Stage E should bridge raw construction and final reveal without jumping to luxury.

---

## 1. Nano Banana Prompt

Use the approved Stage D image as the base image.

Preserve the same waterfall identity.

Preserve the same dark volcanic rock identity.

Preserve the same natural location logic.

Preserve the same lighting direction.

Preserve the same camera logic and scale.

Keep the hidden space visually connected to the waterfall through the entrance, mist, water spray, reflected daylight, or visible falling water near the edge of the frame.

Transform the raw expanded excavation into a habitable but unfinished hidden space. Keep natural volcanic rock walls dominant. Add only basic practical construction progress: a roughly leveled floor, simple waterproof floor layer, minimal rough reinforcement, a dry cleared footprint, and simple unfinished work surfaces. The space should now feel usable, but not completed.

The build should still feel achievable by a single person over time.

The interior must remain raw and unfinished.

Do not add luxury furniture.

Do not add beds.

Do not add sofas.

Do not add a kitchen.

Do not add decorative shelves.

Do not add glass walls.

Do not add premium wood finishes.

Do not add polished surfaces.

Do not add warm final reveal lighting.

Do not make it look like a generic apartment.

Do not remove the natural rock identity.

Do not create a fully finished room.

The final image should show a rough hidden space behind the waterfall that is becoming habitable but remains unfinished, practical, damp, and under construction.

---

## 2. Validation Checklist

- Stage D waterfall identity remains connected
- Natural dark volcanic rock remains dominant
- Space is more usable than Stage D
- Floor is roughly leveled or partially waterproofed
- Construction progress is visible
- Space feels habitable but unfinished
- Scale feels achievable by one person over time
- No luxury furniture
- No beds
- No sofas
- No kitchen
- No glass walls
- No premium wood finishes
- No polished surfaces
- No final reveal lighting
- No generic apartment look
- Natural rock identity remains strong

---

## 3. Failure Modes

### Sudden Luxury Jump

The model adds finished furniture, beds, sofas, kitchen, premium wood, glass walls, polished floors, warm luxury lighting, or decor.

### Generic Interior

The image stops feeling like a hidden waterfall dwelling and becomes a normal room or apartment.

### Overbuilt Architecture

The model adds too many walls, beams, stairs, doors, windows, panels, or modern architectural systems.

### Underbuilt Space

The image remains only raw excavation and does not feel habitable yet.

### Natural Context Loss

The waterfall connection, volcanic rock, mist, or natural context disappears.

### Scale Jump

The space becomes too large, too clean, or impossible for one person to have built gradually.

---

## 4. Retry Prompt

Use the previous image as the base image.

Keep the waterfall identity, volcanic rock, entrance relationship, mist, water spray, lighting direction, camera logic, and scale consistent.

Remove luxury furniture, beds, sofas, kitchen, glass walls, premium wood finishes, polished surfaces, final reveal lighting, and decorative objects.

Show a habitable but unfinished construction state only.

Keep natural rock walls dominant.

Add a roughly leveled floor, simple waterproofing, dry cleared footprint, minimal rough reinforcement, and practical unfinished work surfaces.

The space should feel usable but still under construction.

The final image should bridge raw excavation and completed dwelling without becoming a finished luxury room.
