# Episode002 - Cliffside Workshop

## 1. Concept

A hidden workshop carved into a massive cliff by a lone craftsman.

The episode follows one person gradually transforming an untouched cliff face into a believable handcrafted living and working space. The build should feel physically possible, slow, intentional, and shaped by manual labor rather than instant construction.

## 2. Theme

"Building a home where nobody expects one."

## 3. Final Vision

The completed cliff house should feel hidden, practical, warm, and handcrafted.

Key visual elements:

- natural rock entrance
- wooden door
- small wooden terrace
- warm lantern light
- cliff view
- stone and timber construction
- realistic scale
- handcrafted appearance

The final reveal should make the viewer feel that the structure belongs inside the cliff rather than being placed on top of it.

## 4. Story Structure

### Stage A - Untouched Cliff

Wide shot of untouched cliff.

### Stage B - Rock Crack Discovery

Discover a narrow rock crack.

### Stage C - Surface Clearing

Clear vegetation and loose stones.

### Stage D - Initial Digging

Begin digging with pickaxe.

### Stage E - Chamber Expansion

Remove rocks and expand the chamber.

### Stage F - Timber Support

Install timber beams.

### Stage G - Structure Build

Build floor, walls and entrance.

### Stage H - Interior Furnishing

Furnish with lanterns, shelves and stove.

### Stage I - Sunset Reveal

Sunset reveal of the finished hidden home.

## 5. Production Goal

This episode establishes the standard construction workflow for all future episodes.

The production should emphasize:

- realism
- manual craftsmanship
- clear step-by-step construction
- believable human-scale progress
- cinematic storytelling
- natural integration with the environment

The goal is to create a reusable episode format where each stage shows one understandable construction progression, building toward a satisfying final reveal without sacrificing physical believability.
