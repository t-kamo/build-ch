# AI Construction Video Editing

## Summary

Editing should make the construction sequence clear, fast, and satisfying. The edit is not meant to hide a weak process with heavy effects. It should preserve chronological construction logic and remove anything that slows retention.

CapCut is the final assembly tool.

## Rules

### Editing Workflow

The edit should preserve the construction logic while increasing pace and clarity.

1. Assemble clips in chronological construction order.
2. Trim weak frames.
3. Speed up slow process sections.
4. Strengthen the first 2 seconds.
5. Give the final reveal enough screen time.
6. Export as a clean vertical short-form video.

### CapCut Workflow

- Import all generated clips.
- Place clips in construction order.
- Remove weak beginnings and endings.
- Speed up slow sections while preserving readability.
- Keep transitions minimal or absent.
- Export as vertical short-form video.

### Clip Assembly

- Assemble scene by scene.
- Start with the strongest hook frame.
- Keep every clip connected to the previous construction state.
- Do not reorder clips for aesthetics if it breaks physical logic.

### Speed Control

- Most process phases should feel fast.
- Individual phases often work best when compressed to roughly 1-2 seconds if the action remains readable.
- Slow down only when clarity or reveal satisfaction requires it.

### Scene Duration

- Target final video length: about 35-45 seconds for a full construction story.
- Shorter videos can work for simple builds.
- Longer videos need constant visible progress.
- Remove dead frames, idle worker moments, and unclear motion.

### Hook Optimization

- The opening must show the mystery immediately.
- Use a strong before frame: empty land, abandoned building, hidden hatch, marked zone, or impossible location.
- Avoid slow intros, title cards, or explanatory setup.
- The first clip should make viewers ask what is being built.

### Final Reveal Optimization

- Give the final reveal enough time to register.
- Show the completed hidden space clearly.
- Use a cleaner, calmer shot after the fast construction sequence.
- End on a strong final frame that can loop back to the ordinary starting location.

### Export Rules

- Export vertical 9:16.
- Use the highest practical resolution available.
- Keep the image clean and avoid excessive filters.
- Preserve natural construction audio or add subtle process-focused sound if needed.

### Platform Publishing Rules

- Publish the same final video across YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, TikTok, and Facebook.
- Track which hook, location, and reveal types perform best.
- Use winners as series seeds.
- Do not judge the format from one upload.

## Checklist

- [ ] Are clips in correct construction order?
- [ ] Does every second show progress or reveal value?
- [ ] Is the opening hook immediate?
- [ ] Are weak frames trimmed?
- [ ] Is speed fast but still readable?
- [ ] Are transitions minimal?
- [ ] Is the final reveal clear and satisfying?
- [ ] Is the video exported vertically for short-form platforms?

## Examples

### 40-second Episode Shape

- 0-2s: hook location and secret build goal
- 2-8s: marking and clearing
- 8-16s: excavation and structure
- 16-24s: cover, hatch, camouflage
- 24-34s: interior construction layers
- 34-42s: final hidden base reveal

### Editing Mistake

Adding dramatic transitions between every clip.

Why it fails: the viewer loses the feeling of one continuous construction process.

## Reusable Framework

Use this editing pass:

1. Assembly pass: place clips in physical order.
2. Cleanup pass: trim dead frames and failed motion.
3. Speed pass: compress slow construction steps.
4. Hook pass: strengthen the first 2 seconds.
5. Reveal pass: give the final result enough clarity.
6. Export pass: vertical, clean, platform-ready.
7. Distribution pass: post everywhere and track performance.
